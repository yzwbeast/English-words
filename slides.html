001 define —— 明确“定义”

  • define，[动] 下定义；定范围。

    The concept is hard to define. 很难给这个概念下定义。

    define one's duties 规定某人的职责

    These works are difficult to define one overall style. 这些作品难以界定一个总体的风格。

  • definition，[名] 定义；清晰度。

    I need a definition of this word. 我需要这个词的定义。

    The photo lacks definition. 这张照片不够清晰。

    What's your definition of happiness. 你对幸福的定义是什么？

  • identify，[动] 鉴定；识别；确认。

    I tried to identify her perfume. 我试着分辨出她用的哪种香水。

    It's difficult to identify too many similar products. 鉴别出这么多类似的产品是很困难的。

    I identify that stolen recorder as mine. 我认出那个被偷的录音机是我的。

001 define —— 明确“定义”

  • identifiable，[形] 可识别的；可辨认的。

    The picture is not identifiable. 这张图片无法识别。

    The statue is identifiable as a goddess. 这个石像可辨认是一尊女神像。

    He is easily identifiable by his strange hat. 他那奇怪的帽子让人一下子就能认出他来。

  • identity，[名] 身份；特性；一致。

    Identity of interests is the bond that unties them. 利益一致是把他们联合起来的纽带。

    identity card 身份证

    to foist a common national identity on new citizens 对新的居民强加民族共同特性

  • determine，[动] 决心；决定；测定；确定。

    This examination will determine your final grade. 这次考试将决定你的最终成绩。

    determine on giving up smoking 决心戒烟

    to determine the velocity 测定速度

001 define —— 明确“定义”

  • determinism，[名] 宿命论；决定论。

    If you believe in determinism, you have to believe it all the way. 如果你信仰决定论，你就必须毫无保留地相信。

    I don't believe in historical determinism. 我不相信历史决定论。

    Whorf came to believe in a sort of linguistic determinism. 沃尔夫开始信奉一种语言决定论。【04年 S3 阅读 Part B #3-4】

  • judge，[名] 法官；裁判 [动] 判断；评判；审判。

    She's a good judge of wine. 她是鉴别酒的专家。

    Don't judge by appearances. 不能以貌取人。

    I judge him to be about 40. 我断定他有40来岁。

002 behavior —— 注意自己的每个“行为”和“举止”

  • behavior，[名] 举止；表现。

    He speaks very highly of the boy's behavior. 他高度评价这个男孩的表现。

    childish behavior 幼稚的行为

    They observed the behavior of wild animals. 他们观察野生动物的生活习性。

  • behavioral，[形] 行为的。

    behavioral genetics 行为遗传学

    behavioral psychology 行为心理学

    These issues all have root causes in human behavior: all require behavioral change and social innovations, as well as technological development. 这些问题都是人类行为的根本原因：都需要行为改变和社会创新，以及技术发展。【13年 S2 阅读 Part B #A-1】

  • conduct，[动] 实施；表现；指挥；引导；传导（电、热） [名] 行为，举止；实施方式。

    He has to learn how to conduct business. 他得学习如何经营生意。

    His moral conduct was praised by people. 他的德行受到人们的称赞。

    The students conduct themselves better than expected. 学生们比预料的还要表现得好。

002 behavior —— 注意自己的每个“行为”和“举止”

  • manner，[名] 方式；举止；礼节。

    He is graceful in manner. 他举止优雅。

    table manners 餐桌上的礼节

    Why are you talking in such a strange manner? 你为什么用这种奇怪的方式谈话？

  • performance，[名] 表演；表现；性能；履行；执行。

    Proposal is easier than performance. 建议容易实行难。

    How is the performance of the new car? 这辆新车的性能怎么样？

    How do you evaluate your performance? 你如何评价自己的表现？

003 demand —— 为自己的“主张”发声

  • demand，[名] 要求；请求；需求 [动] 要求；请求；需求。

    The workers demand a definite answer. 工人们要求一个明确的答复。

    They demand prompt payment. 他们要求立即付款。

    The supply is not adequate to the demand. 供不应求。

    语气强度：command(命令)>demand(要求)>require(需要)>request(请求)

  • require，[动] 需要；要求。

    These pets require a lot of care and attention. 这些宠物需要悉心照顾。

    require urgently 迫切需要

    Most plants require sunlight. 大部分植物需要光。

  • claim，[名] 声称；索要；要求；认领 [动] 声称；要求；索赔。

    This illness can claim a child's life. 这种病能夺走一个孩子的生命。

    claim a reward 索求回报

    They claim to have discovered a cure for the disease. 他们声称已经发现了治疗此病的一种药物。

003 demand —— 为自己的“主张”发声

  • acclaim，[名] 赞扬；欢呼 [动] 称赞；欢呼。

    to acclaim the new king 欢呼拥戴新国王

    The novel received great acclaim. 这本小说备受赞扬。

    He acknowledged the acclaim of the crowd. 他对群众的称誉表示感谢。

  • claimant，[名] 要求者；索偿人；原告。

    The compensation will be given to the claimant. 索赔人将得到赔偿金。

    The claimant claims compensation. 原告要求赔偿。

    Instead, the claimant receives a time-limited allowance, conditional on actively seeking a job; no entitlement and no insurance. 取而代之的是，申请人获得一种具有时限、附带积极寻找工作的条件的津贴；没有权利，没有保障。【考研一 2014 S2 阅读 Part A Text 1 #4-5】

  • command，[名] 命令；指挥；掌握 [动] 命令；指挥；掌握。

    obey the command 服从命令

    She has a large English vocabulary at her command. 她能熟练掌握大量词汇。

    The army is under the King's direct command. 军队由国王直接指挥。

003 demand —— 为自己的“主张”发声

  • assert，[动] 断言；宣称；坚持；维护。

    They assert their rights to disagree. 他们坚持自己的否定权。

    They assert that he was innocent. 他们断言，他当时是无罪的。

    He was never able to assert himself sufficiently. 他从来都不能充分地维护自己的权利。

  • assertion，[名] 断言；主张；声明。

    He repeated his assertion that he was not guilty. 他再三声明自己是无罪的。

    He supports this assertion with evidence. 他用证据支持他的主张。

    He insisted his assertion. 他坚持他的主张。

  • request，[名] 请求；要求的事 [动] 要求；请求。

    She made a request for some water. 她请求给点水。

    I request him to come before ten. 我要求他十点以前来。

    reasonable request 合理的要求

003 demand —— 为自己的“主张”发声

  • affirm，[动] 断言；肯定；证实。

    to affirm a view 证明某种观点

    I can affirm that no one will lose their jobs. 我可以肯定，谁都不会丢掉工作。

    I affirm that what he said is true. 我可以肯定他所说的是实情。

  • allegation，[名] （无证据的）说法；指控。

    The lawyer's allegation was proved. 律师的指控已被证明属实。

    allegation of malpractice 玩忽职守的指控

    The company has denied the allegation. 公司否认了这项指控。

004 ignore —— 请选择“忽视”

  • ignore，[动] 忽视；忽略；置之不理。

    If you ignore your diet, trouble will follow. 如果你忽视自己的饮食，麻烦会随之而来。

    Nobody could ignore these problems. 没有谁会忽视这些问题。

    You should ignore their advice. 你需要忽略他们的意见。

  • ignorant，[形] 无知的；缺乏知识的。

    He is ignorant of even the most elementary facts. 他连最基本的事实都不知道。

    make an ignorant mistake 犯了一个无知的错误

    He is totally ignorant of social customs. 他对社会习俗一无所知。

  • neglect，[动] 疏忽；忽视；不予重视 [名] 疏忽；大意。

    You shouldn't neglect your health. 你不该忽视你的健康。

    The garden was in a state of neglect. 那花园处于无人照管的状态。

    He was dismissed for neglect of duty. 他因玩忽职守而被开除了。

004 ignore —— 请选择“忽视”

  • overlook，[动] 忽略；俯视。

    overlook one problem 忽视了一个问题

    The role of talent tends to be overlooked. 天赋的作用容易被忽视。【考研 2007 S2 阅读 Part A Text 1 #Q[24]】

    a kind of overlooked inequality 一种被人们忽视的不平等现象 【考研 2004 S3 阅读 Part A Text 2 #Q[46]】

005 own —— 此物被我“占有”

  • own，[动] 拥有 [形] 自己的。

    Who owns the house? 谁拥有这个房子？

    on my own 靠自己

    She lives on her own. 她一个人过。

  • ownership，[名] 所有权；物主身份。

    The ownership of the house is still unknown. 这栋房子的所有权至今无人知道。

    ownership of land 土地所有权

    He denied the ownership of the dog. 他否认这只狗是他的。

  • property，[名] 所有物；财产；属性；性质。

    private property 私人财产

    All elastic materials possess this property to a certain degree. 所有弹性材料在一定程度上都具有这种特性。

    He has a property in the suburbs. 他在郊区有一处房地产。

005 own —— 此物被我“占有”

  • monopoly，[名] 垄断。

    monopoly price 垄断价格

    Liberty and monopoly cannot live together. 自由与垄断不能并存。

    monopoly enterprise 垄断企业

  • possession，[名] 拥有；财产。

    He had lost his home and all his possessions in the war. 他在战争中失去了自己的家园和所有的财物。

    The possession of wealth does not always bring happiness. 拥有财富并不总是带来幸福。

    He gained possession of more land. 他拥有了更多的土地。

  • possess，[动] 拥有；占有。

    Some animals possess the characteristic of man. 有些动物具有人类的特征。

    possess fortune legally 合法拥有财产

    To possess wealth is not always to be happy. 拥有财富并不总是幸福的。

005 own —— 此物被我“占有”

  • occupy，[动] 占据；占有；占领；使忙碌。

    These people occupy an inferior position in local society. 这些人在当地社会居于下等地位。

    We do not seek to occupy other nations. 我们绝不会占领另一个国家。

    Your family life could occupy most of your time. 你的家庭生活会占据你大部分的时间的。

  • occupation，[名] 占领；占有；职业；消遣。

    Riding is her favorite occupation. 骑马是她最喜欢的消遣。

    occupation rate of market 市场占有率

    a painful reminder of the occupation of what was once a sovereign nation 对这个曾经的主权国家被占领的痛苦提醒 【考研一 2017 S2 阅读 Part A Text 2 #3-2】
